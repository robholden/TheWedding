Welcome to Kerala, India

Located in the southern part of India, Kerala is a lush, tropical state known for its serene backwaters, spice plantations, and a rich cultural heritage. Often called “God’s Own Country,” it’s a land of coconut palms, classical music, vibrant festivals, and some of the most scenic landscapes in the country.

On Kerala’s western coast lies Kochi — a historic port city where cultures have mingled for centuries. Once a key hub in the global spice trade, Kochi has been shaped by Portuguese, Dutch, and British influences, still visible today in its colonial-era architecture, old churches, and winding streets.

Today, Kochi is a laid-back blend of the past and present — with seaside charm, art galleries, fresh seafood, and stunning sunsets over the Arabian Sea. It's the perfect place to experience both the soul of Kerala and the warmth of its people.

Jew Town In Fort Kochi

Renowned for its antique shops, vibrant spice markets, and the iconic Paradesi Synagogue, it offers a unique glimpse into Kochi's Jewish heritage.

The Spice Markets In Fort Kochi

Kochi has been a spice trading hub for centuries. The spice markets are a sensory delight, filled with the aroma of cardamom, pepper, and other spices. You can buy fresh spices and learn about their significance in Kerala's cuisine.

Explore the traditional vegetarian feast of Sadya, featuring up to 28 dishes that showcase the breadth of Kerala's vegetarian cuisine.
